💡 Key Findings
1

RL outperforms traditional methods. Both Q-Learning and Value Iteration significantly reduce waiting times compared to Random and Fixed Timer baselines, proving that intelligent signal control is superior to rule-based approaches.

2

Model-free vs model-based tradeoff. Value Iteration gives the optimal solution but requires full knowledge of traffic dynamics. Q-Learning learns from experience alone, making it more practical for real-world deployment.

3

Q-Learning achieves near-optimal performance. With ~84% of Value Iteration's performance, Q-Learning demonstrates that model-free methods can approach optimal solutions through sufficient exploration and learning.

4

Reward engineering matters. The switch penalty prevents unrealistic rapid signal flickering, modeling the real-world yellow-light delay. Without it, the agent would switch every step.
